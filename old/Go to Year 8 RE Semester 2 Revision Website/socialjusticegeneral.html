Works of Mercy

The seven works of mercy are as such:
1. Admonish Sinners; Warn sinners of their wrong-doing so that they may change.
2. Instruct the Uninformed; Teaching children about God and Christianity.
3. Counsel the Doubtful; Give advice to people in doubt or in a difficult situation.
4. Comfort the Sorrowful; Provide aid and comfort to people when they feel bad for their previous sins.
5. Be Patient with those in Error; Be patient with sinners, as change will come in God's own time.
6. Forgive Offences; Forgive the sins of others.
7. Pray for the Living and the Dead; Pray for All.



What is Social Justice

Social Justice is the fair treatment and equitable status of all individuals and social groups within a society. Social Justice appears in many forms, such as a public act of deed. An example of this is a phenomina called "preferential treatment of the poor," where poorer people are given preference over more fortunate people. Another example is showing hospitality to strangers, where you show friendliness and generousity to others, no matter what relationship you have with them.
