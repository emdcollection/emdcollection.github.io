Christian Symbols

Below, you will find some symbols related to Christianity.

1. Cross. Most likely the most popular Christian symbol, the Cross generally represents Christianity.

2. The Crucifixion. Usually depicted as Jesus on a Cross, which was mentioned before, The Crucifixion represents Jesus' sacrifice for us. It represents Jesus triumph over sins.

3. Ichthus. Represented by a fish-like icon, the Ichthus is a symbol associated with Christianity. It used to be used to tell other Christians their place was a safespace for Christians. It means Jesus (I = Jesus, Ch = Christ, Th = Theos (God), U = Son Of, S = Saviour

4. Dove. The dove represents peace, and many variations of the dove, such as a burning dove, can also represent the Holy Spirit.

5. Alpha and Omega. Being the first and last letters of the Greek Alphabet, the Alpha and Omega represent Jesus; The Beginning and The End.

6. The Lamb. The Lamb, sometimes depicted with a halo around its head, represents innocence and purity. It also represents the sacrifice Jesus made for us.

7. Flame. A flame represents the Holy Spirit. When the disciples were given the ability to speak in different tounges, a Flame lit over their heads, signalling that they had the Holy Spirit.

8. Wedding Rings. The Wedding Rings represent faithfulness and unending love. It is attributed to the Sacrament of Marriage.

9. Holy Trinity. The Holy Trinity is usually represented as a triangle, or three lops connected to each other. It represents the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit.

10. Bread and Wine. The Bread and Wine are both key symbols in the Eucharist. The Bread and Wine represent the Body and Blood of Christ respectively.
