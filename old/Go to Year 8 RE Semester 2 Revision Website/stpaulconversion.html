St Paul Conversion

The Road to Damascus

After Paul reports to authorities about some christians and requests that he can go after them, Paul assembles a small groups of Pharisees and heads to Damascus. On their journey to Damascus, Paul is encountered by Jesus in the form of a heavenly light, who asks Paul "Why do you persecute me?" After a brief conversation, Jesus blinds Paul by covering his eyes with scales, and gives him instructions for when he arrives at Damascus.



At Damascus

When Paul arrives at Damascus, he spends 3 days praying, without eating. Around this time, a man called Ananias is met by Jesus, still being embodied by a heavenly light. Jesus tells Ananias to meet Paul, but Ananias is initially sceptical, listing all of the bad that Paul had done to Christians, and the fact that he came to Damascus to persecute the Christians. Jesus tells Ananias of Paul's current state and whereabouts, and Ananias comes to Paul. Ananias lays his hands on Paul, which causes the scales covering Paul's eyes to fall, giving Paul his sight back. From this moment onwards, Paul is Christian, and after being healed Paul spreads the word about Christianity and eventually meets the disciples. At first, people are reasonably sceptical due to Paul's past, but they eventually see Paul;s sincerity.
